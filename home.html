Ignyte

Ignyte licenses SDKs that bring motion tracking to mobile devices. This essentially expands the canvas of interaction past the screen, to allow a more natural interface with applications


What began as a 24 hour hack at the StudentRND CodeDaySF Hackathon, with my friend Ash Bhat(@theashbhat), has turned into a project that we're actively expanding.


Although still relatively alpha, it is the most advanced of its kind to the best of our knowledge, and has attracted quite a bit of press.


This project also won the 'Best Application Award' at the CodeDay 2013 February Hackathon, along with an invitation to the Leap Motion offices through Twitter!

TinoConfessions

Cupertino Confessions website allows users to submit a confession in the text box, and the javascript would then log the response anonymously in a Parse database. To complete the picture, I also created an admin panel, which authenticated admins through facebook, and allowed them to perform several actions on each confession such as submit, edit, and delete.


Note: I am not a Cupertino Confessions Admin

WorthIt

During the summer of 2012, I had interned for toMooch Inc., a company that was interested in generating a social network based on unique product reviews.


And so I set out to develop a prototype iOS application that would allow users to essentially take pictures of any product and rate it.


This was an incredible experience, as it taught me how to effectively use the Facebook Graph to my advantage, for automated image transactions, data logging on Facebook Application, as well as social product marketing.

Dusty V Autotargeting System

As the co-project manager for my high school's FIRST robotics team (2473), I led a team that designed the 2012 autotargeting system for our robot.


Used Analytical Geometry, Trigonometry, and a basic computer vision library, I successfully wrote and implemented an algorithm that allowed the robot to detect retro-reflective targets (basketball hoops), as well as the distance and direction toward the target.
